Q: What are the Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings?
A: The THE Rankings evaluate over 2,000 research-intensive universities globally, using a set of 18 performance indicators grouped into five main categories: Teaching, Research Environment, Research Quality, International Outlook, and Industry Income. They provide a balanced and transparent method to benchmark institutions.

Q: What are the main criteria of THE World University Rankings?
A: THE divides its scoring into five pillars:
- **Teaching** (Learning environment – ~30%)
- **Research** (Volume, income & reputation – ~30%)
- **Citations** (Research influence – ~30%)
- **International outlook** (Staff, students, research – ~7.5%)
- **Industry income** (Innovation – ~2.5%)

Q: How is the ‘Teaching’ score calculated?
A: Teaching is evaluated with:
- 15% based on a reputational survey
- 4.5% student-to-staff ratio
- 6% doctorate production
- 2.25% earnings per academic
- 2.25% PhD-to-Bachelor award ratios  
(Total 30%)

Q: What about the ‘Research’ pillar?
A: Research is measured by:
- 18% reputation survey
- 6% research income
- 6% publication volume per staff  
(Total 30%)  
:contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

Q: How do citations influence ranking?
A: Citations, weighted at 30%, measure normalized research impact using global databases like Web of Science, reflecting academic influence.

Q: What factors count for International Outlook?
A: This 7.5% component includes:
- 2.5% proportion of international students
- 2.5% international staff ratio
- 2.5% international research collaboration  
:contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

Q: What does Industry Income measure?
A: Industry income (2.5%) captures the university’s innovation and knowledge transfer by assessing research funding from industry relative to faculty numbers.

Q: Are there exclusion criteria for THE Rankings?
A: Yes. Universities can be excluded if they:
- Publish fewer than 1,000 relevant papers in five years
- Offer no undergraduate degrees
- Report insufficient data

Q: How are THE scores calculated overall?
A: THE uses standardized Z-scores for each metric, combines them into the five pillars, then creates an overall score by weighted summation. Missing data is imputed conservatively.
